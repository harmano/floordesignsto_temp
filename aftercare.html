After Care.

Follow these guides to best care for you project

Caring for your Vinyl

For tough stains, try soaking them for several minutes with very hot, soapy water. The application of some denatured alcohol may also help loosen and remove these stubborn contaminants.

In General.

It’s best to clean only when the surface appears dirty, as overcleaning can result in drying out the vinyl. Any contaminants which are allowed to remain on the graphic too long however will probably be more difficult to remove the longer they sit. For tougher stains, try soaking them for several minutes with very hot, soapy water. The application of some denatured alcohol may also help loosen and remove these stubborn contaminants. If they remain, special cleaning solutions may need to be applied prior to a full wipedown. Do not use rough scrubbing action or abrasive tools, as this will damage the film. When finished removing any contaminants, be sure to immediately wash and rinse off any product residue.

Maintenance is especially important in congested industrial or urban locales or in high-elevation areas. At high elevations, wraps can suffer UV damage much easier than they can at sea level. Because the air is thinner, less filtering is available against the damaging effects of the sun.

In congested industrial or urban locations, increased pollution and smog reduce the resilience of a wrap. Horizontal areas capture environmental chemicals on the wrap’s surface, thus increasing UV exposure and reducing strength.

The best way to clean a vinyl surface is to manually clean the surface with water and a mild detergent. Do not use cleaners that leave a waxy shine as the substance usually contains petroleum distillate – a damaging ingredient. Also, never use battery powered brushes as it can cause scratches or catch and lift the edge of the vinyl.

When hand-cleaning, use soft cloths and sponges with a water and mild detergent mix. The detergent should not contain any abrasive components and have a pH between 3 and 11. It should also be free of alcohol and solvents. Don’t abrade the filmed surface with extra scrubbing. Thoroughly rinse the vinyl with clean water. Either allow the wrap to naturally dry or dab a non-abrasive towel or chamois over the surface. Be careful about not lifting the edges of the wrap’s film.

- PVC films behave similar to any surface and if left in constant contact with dirt, grime and contaminants, it will eventually degrade, especially if left unclean, exposed to UV light and/or heat.

- It is recommended that vinyl should be cleaned on a regular basis or whenever they appear dirty. Ideally, a wrap should be cleaned every 2 weeks. This ensures that your wrap retains optimal visual appearance for the duration of their life.

NOTE:

  • - Be careful to not lift the film edges
  • - Detergent should be free from abrasive components, strong solvents and alcohols
  • - Detergents should have a pH balance between 5 and 9. Always test the detergent in an inconspicuous spot on the vinyl before using

- Using an aftercare vinyl product to care for the wrap film is great for protecting the vinyl wrap from being damaged by environmental pollutants or airborne pollutants.

- NOTE: Make sure the wax used does not contain petroleum distillates or abrasive components. Always test the wax in an inconspicuous area prior to waxing the whole wrap. Any wax residue can be removed with all-purpose cleaner.

Removing Fingerprints.

The matte and satin products may show fingerprints, however they can be cleaned by saturating a clean, lint free towel with a 2:1 mixture of Isopropyl Alcohol and water or straight rubbing alcohol, and then wipe the film dry.


Polish & Wax.

Most standard graphic films and overlaminates can be polished or waxed with a high quality wax. Before use, test and approve in an inconspicuous area. Do not use any abrasive polishes or cutting compounds. For the carbon fibre and brushed metal films in 3M™ Wrap Film Series, a polishing product such as Meguiars® Natural Shine Protectant may be used. We do not recommend any polishing or wax product for the matt or textured films.
